An alternate scheme (Fig Figure 5.2.3, left) plots points in the plane and considers the fraction of points that fall below the curve (i.e., ‘the acceptance region‘). This fraction is multiplied by the total area of the region (easier to calculate since the shape is likely square). Though this is easier to program than the notation might suggest, consider the indicator function
\begin{equation*} I(i) = \begin{cases}1, \quad y_i < f(x_i)\\0, \quad\text{otherwise}\end{cases} \end{equation*}
 and take 
\begin{equation*} \int_a^b f(x)\,dx \approx(f_{max}-f_{min})(b-a)\dfrac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N I(i) \end{equation*}
