Delbert prepares a \(20\%\) glucose solution of by mixing \(2\) mL of glucose with \(8\) mL of water. If he adds \(x\) ml of glucose to the solution, its concentration is given by
\begin{equation*} C(x) = \frac{2 + x}{8+x} \end{equation*}
  1. How many milliliters of glucose should Delbert add to increase the concentration to \(50\%\text{?}\)
  2. Graph the function on the domain \([0, 100]\text{.}\)
  3. What is the horizontal asymptote of the graph? What does it tell you about the solution?
